The Softest Glow

A Little Lanterns Lane Story of Quiet Courage

Ages 6–8 · 32-page 8.5×8.5″ paperback

Soft is still light.

Mira’s new night-lantern won’t blaze like the others—only a shy circle of light. She tries to force it brighter until the glow goes out. With Grandma’s help, she names what is true: homesick, mad, curious, brave-enough. The soft glow returns, and when neighbors visit, quiet light helps everyone settle.

A warm bedtime picture book about quiet belonging—where a shy night-lantern teaches that soft light is still brave light.

Inside the book

Four quiet beats from Mira’s first night on the lane.

  1. A shy circle of light. A new room, a tea-colored lantern, and a glow that feels too small beside the neighbors’.
  2. Forced brighter—then gone. Trying to make the lantern blaze only flickers the soft glow out.
  3. Naming what is true. With Grandma’s help: homesick, mad, curious, brave-enough.
  4. Soft light that stays. When Leo and Samira visit, quiet light helps the forts hush and the stories settle.

For caregivers

A read-aloud note

Kids love saying the refrain along: Soft is still light. After the last page, try the optional prompt from the back matter:

Tonight we can… Name one soft feeling before lights out.

Keep it short—closeness, not a lesson plan. More pacing tips live on the caregivers page.
