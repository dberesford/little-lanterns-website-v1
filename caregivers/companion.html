Read-aloud companion

For The Softest Glow — ages 6–8. Soft is still light.

Back to tips

Tonight’s tips

  • Before you begin: notice the amber rim on the cover — glow can be quiet and still true.
  • When the light feels shy: pause on the soft circle. Ask what they notice. Never call the lantern failed.
  • At the settle: slow your voice; leave a beat after the last line.

Pace · pause · questions

  • Pace — match the art; night scenes take an extra breath.
  • Pause — after a feeling-heavy spread, wait before turning.
  • Questions — invite, don’t quiz: Where would you stand? What feels warm here?

Discussion prompts

  1. When have you felt shy and still wanted to shine?
  2. What is the difference between a loud glow and a soft one?
  3. Who helps Mira feel less alone — and how?
  4. If your lantern had a color tonight, what would it be?
  5. What would you whisper to Mira after the last page?
